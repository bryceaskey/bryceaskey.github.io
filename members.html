Current members

Jeongim Kim

Principal investigator

Dr. Kim is an assistant professor in the Horticultural Sciences Department and a member of the Plant Molecular and Cellular Biology (PMCB) graduate program. She recieved her PhD at Purdue University studying auxin biosynthesis and plant abiotic stress physiology. During her post-doctoral training at Purdue, she studied phenylpropanoid biosynthesis and engaged in several projects aiming to increase biofuel production. She is interested in understanding molecular mechanisms underlying the integration of metabolic networks and plant stress responses and/or growth.

Veronica Perez

Graduate student

Veronica is a PhD candidate of the Plant Molecular and Cell Biology program. Her interests lie in plant secondary metabolism, and specifically in understanding the role of amino acid-derived metabolites known as aldoximes. Currently she is studying the role and mechanisms of aldoxime-derived auxin biosynthesis in several model plant species as well as characterizing the outcomes of aldoxime metabolism in non-Brassicales species. When not in the lab, she spends her time walking, listening to podcasts, and playing with her cat.

Bryce Askey

Undergraduate student

Bryce is an undergraduate student pursuing a dual degree in Biological Engineering and Horticultural Science. He is interested in applying systems biology methods to further our understanding of plant biochemistry, and especially its underlying regulatory mechanisms. Currently he is working with a variety of Scutellaria species to characterize their diversity in accumulation of medicinally active metabolites. Outside of the lab, he enjoys biking around Gainesville, cooking, and baking sourdough bread.
